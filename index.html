The research journey

From initial observations to final product ‚Äî GR1 through GR7

GR1

Initial Research

Early interviews revealed a consistent pattern: people love taking photos but hate the aftermath. Manual organization feels like homework.

KEY QUOTE

"I just send everything and hope someone keeps it."

OUTCOME

Identified chaos in memory sharing. Observed reluctance to organize photos post-event due to time constraints and overwhelming volume.

GR2

Refined Interviews

Narrowed focus to college students and young professionals. Discovered that sharing happens primarily after social events ‚Äî parties, trips, campus gatherings.

KEY INSIGHT

Users share mostly after: parties (65%), trips (48%), and campus events (37%). Event-based behavior is the core pattern.

OUTCOME

Defined target user and validated event-based sharing as the primary use case worth solving for.

GR3

Problem Framing

Synthesized research into a clear problem statement that would guide all design decisions moving forward.

HMW STATEMENT

How might we make sharing event photos effortless and intelligent without requiring manual sorting?

OUTCOME

Defined core user (college students), core scenario (post-event sharing), and emotional goal (preserve moments without friction).

GR4

Ideation & Concepts

Explored multiple approaches including manual tagging, QR code check-ins, collaborative albums, and AI grouping. Tested various interaction models.

CONCEPTS EXPLORED

Manual tagging (rejected: too slow), QR check-in (rejected: friction), collaborative creation (rejected: coordination overhead), automatic grouping (selected).

OUTCOME

Automatic grouping won because it removed all user effort while delivering immediate value. Zero-friction was the deciding factor.

GR5

Prototype

Built low-fidelity wireframes to test core interactions. Focused on upload flow, grouping visualization, and sharing mechanisms.

TESTING FEEDBACK

"Wait, it already organized my photos? That's exactly what I needed." Users loved the speed but wanted transparency in how grouping worked.

OUTCOME

Validated core flow. Identified need for: (1) confirmation step before sharing, (2) visual explanation of grouping logic, (3) edit capability.

GR6

Street Testing

Observed real users with actual event photos. Watched behavior in authentic contexts rather than controlled lab settings.

KEY OBSERVATION

Users loved automation but wanted control. They appreciated smart grouping but needed ability to adjust before sending. Trust required transparency.

OUTCOME

Added review step with clear grouping rationale. Users now see why photos were grouped and can adjust before sharing.

GR7

Final Iteration

Refined UI based on all learnings. Improved onboarding, added smart confirmation step, and enhanced transparency around AI decisions.

FINAL IMPROVEMENTS

Added: onboarding with privacy explanation, grouping preview with edit options, clear visual indicators for AI confidence, one-tap sharing after confirmation.

OUTCOME

Reduced user confusion by 78%. Increased perceived intelligence while maintaining trust. Users felt in control while benefiting from automation.
